Citizenship Fingerprint Request from Outside Canada: A Complete Guide

Dec 29, 2025 • 5 min read

When applying for Canadian citizenship, a background check is a crucial requirement. In certain cases, this includes submitting fingerprints to Canadian authorities. For applicants living outside Canada, the fingerprinting process may seem overwhelming at first—but with the right information, it is simple and efficient.

This guide explains the purpose, eligibility, step-by-step process, and costs of submitting a Citizenship Fingerprint Request from outside Canada, helping you complete the requirement confidently and without delays.

Purpose of a Citizenship Fingerprint Request

A Citizenship Fingerprint Request is required for some applicants as part of the citizenship application review process. Its main purposes include:

  • Identity Verification: Fingerprints help confirm the applicant’s identity.
  • Criminal Background Check: The Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP) uses fingerprints to check for any criminal record.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) may request fingerprints to ensure all legal and security requirements are met.

Who Is Required to Submit Fingerprints?

IRCC will notify applicants directly if fingerprints are required. This typically applies in cases such as:

  • Applicants with common or similar names
  • Individuals who have lived in multiple countries
  • Situations where additional identity verification is necessary

Step-by-Step Process to Submit Fingerprints from Outside Canada

1. Receive the Fingerprint Request Letter

The process begins once you receive an official fingerprint request letter from IRCC outlining submission instructions and timelines.

2. Choose an Accredited Fingerprinting Agency

Select an RCMP-accredited agency or a recognized international fingerprinting provider that meets Canadian standards for digital or ink fingerprints.

3. Book Your Appointment

Most fingerprinting agencies offer online or phone booking, making it easy to schedule an appointment at your convenience.

4. Prepare Required Documents

Bring the following documents to your appointment:

  • Fingerprint request letter from IRCC
  • Valid government-issued photo ID (passport recommended)
  • Any additional forms required by the fingerprinting agency
5. Have Your Fingerprints Taken

Fingerprints may be captured digitally or using ink, depending on the agency’s setup. Ensure the prints are clear and meet RCMP specifications.

6. Submit Fingerprints to the RCMP

The agency may submit fingerprints electronically to the RCMP or provide you with the required documents for mailing. Always follow the instructions provided in your IRCC request letter.

7. Track Your Submission

Keep your receipt or tracking number to monitor your fingerprint submission and respond promptly if IRCC requests further information.

Cost of Citizenship Fingerprinting Outside Canada

Costs can vary depending on location and service provider and may include:

  • Fingerprinting Fees: Typically CAD 50–150
  • Courier Charges: If physical documents must be mailed
  • Additional Service Fees: Document verification or certification, if required

Tips for a Smooth Fingerprinting Experience

  • Choose an experienced and reputable fingerprinting agency
  • Double-check documentation before your appointment
  • Book early to avoid missing IRCC deadlines
  • Respond quickly to any follow-up communication from IRCC
