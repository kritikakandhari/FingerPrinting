Navigating Name Change Applications in BC

Oct 20, 2025
6 min read

Changing your legal name is a significant milestone, whether it's due to marriage, divorce, or personal choice. In British Columbia, the process is structured but requires careful attention to detail. At Point Zero Global Services, we help dozens of clients every week navigate these waters.

1. Eligibility Requirements

To apply for a name change in BC, you must be 19 years of age or older (different rules apply for minors) and have lived in British Columbia for at least three months immediately prior to the date of your application.

2. The Fingerprinting Requirement

This is where most people get stuck. If you are 18 years or older, you **must** have your fingerprints taken. These fingerprints are used by the RCMP to conduct a criminal record check to ensure the name change isn't being used to evade legal obligations.

**Pro Tip:** Your fingerprints must be taken by an RCMP-accredited agency like Point Zero. We use digital scanning to ensure high quality and fast processing.

3. Necessary Documentation

  • All original birth certificates (including any previous name change certificates).
  • A valid government-issued photo ID.
  • The completed Legal Change of Name Application form.

4. Fees & Processing

The current fee for a name change is $137 CAD, which includes one change of name certificate. Fingerprinting fees are separate and depend on the service provider.
